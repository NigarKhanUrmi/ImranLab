About the Lab

Advancing Health Across the Lifespan

The Cardio-DREAM Laboratory is an applied research facility within the Department of Movement Sciences focusing on sleep and circadian behaviors.

The purpose of the Cardio-DREAM Laboratory is to advance understanding of how circadian and sleep behaviors influence cardiovascular, neural, and metabolic health across the lifespan, with the goal of informing evidence-based strategies for chronic disease prevention.

Interdisciplinary Collaboration

Our laboratory is a collaborative space that brings together faculty and students from various disciplines across the university. We support hands-on learning for undergraduate and graduate students in exercise science, health science, nutritional science, and medical education programs.

Primary Research Focus

Our research examines how sleep loss, extension, quality, and napping habits affect cardiovascular and metabolic health. We integrate innovative nutrition, exercise behavior, and technological interventions to influence brain activity, inflammation, and cardiometabolic disease risk factors.

State-of-the-Art Equipment

Our facility is equipped with precision tools for comprehensive physiological and chronobiological assessment.

Sleep & Chronobiology

Featuring Polysomnography sleep testing, EEG for brain activity, and GeneActive devices to assess light exposure, physical activity, and sleep timing.

Metabolic & Blood Analysis

Equipped with a Piccolo Express blood analyzer, Metabolic analyzer, and Inbody Dial H20 for body composition and insulin resistance studies.

Cardiovascular Assessment

Utilizing Arteriograph technology to measure vascular function alongside traditional Treadmills and Cycle ergometers for exercise stress testing.

Clinical Metrics

Standardized measurement with Hand Grip Dynamometers and Seca 703 medical scales for precise height and weight data across the lifespan.
